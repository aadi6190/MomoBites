Veg Momos :

These momos typically include a filling of finely chopped mixed vegetables such as cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, onions, and sometimes mushrooms. The filling is seasoned with spices like garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and often a hint of green chili for a bit of heat. Preparation: The vegetable filling is saut√©ed lightly with the spices and sauces until cooked yet still slightly crunchy. This mixture is then used to fill small rounds of dough made from all-purpose flour or a mixture of wheat and refined flour. Cooking Method: The momos are then shaped into small dumplings, usually pleated at the top to seal in the filling, and steamed until the dough becomes translucent and cooked. They are typically served hot with a spicy tomato-based dipping sauce or a tangy, slightly sweet momo sauce.

Paneer Momos :

Ingredients: Paneer momos are made with a filling of crumbled or finely chopped paneer, along with onions, garlic, ginger, and sometimes green peas or spinach for added flavor and texture. Spices like cumin, coriander, garam masala, and a touch of chili powder are used to season the filling. Preparation: The paneer and vegetable mixture is cooked together until the flavors meld and the paneer is slightly browned. This filling is then used to stuff the momo dough. Cooking Method: The paneer momos are shaped and steamed just like vegetable momos. They are often served with a mint-coriander chutney or a yogurt-based dipping sauce, which complements the mild yet flavorful paneer filling.

Chicken Momos :

Ingredients: Chicken momos feature a filling made from minced or finely chopped chicken mixed with onions, garlic, ginger, and sometimes spring onions or cilantro for freshness. Spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and a bit of chili powder or chopped green chilies are used to season the chicken filling. Preparation: The chicken mixture is cooked until the meat is tender and fully cooked, absorbing the flavors of the spices and aromatics. Cooking Method: Similar to vegetable and paneer momos, the chicken filling is then used to stuff the momo dough, which is steamed until the dough is cooked and the filling is juicy and flavorful. Chicken momos are often served with a spicy chili sauce or a tangy sesame-based dipping sauce.
